Etsy Seller Fees Explained (2026)

Etsy charges three types of fees on every sale. Understanding these helps you price your products correctly and protect your profit margin.

Frequently Asked Questions About Etsy Fees

Etsy charges three fees per sale: a $0.20 listing fee, a 6.5% transaction fee on the total sale price including shipping, and a payment processing fee of 3% + $0.25. On a $25 item with $5 shipping, your total Etsy fees would be around $3.28.
Etsy's transaction fee is 6.5% of the total sale amount including shipping and gift wrap charges. This was increased from 5% to 6.5% in April 2022 and remains at 6.5% in 2026.
Yes. Etsy charges $0.20 to list each item, regardless of whether it sells. Each listing is active for 4 months. If it doesn't sell, you pay $0.20 again to renew it. If it does sell, the listing automatically renews at $0.20 per item sold.
A healthy Etsy profit margin is generally 30–50% after all fees and costs. Margins below 15% are considered low. Our calculator shows a green indicator for margins above 15% and yellow for lower margins, so you can quickly see where you stand.

Tips to Improve Your Etsy Profit Margin

💰

Price for profit, not just sales

Use this calculator before setting any price. Many Etsy sellers underprice and don't realize they're making a loss after fees.

📦

Charge realistic shipping

Etsy's transaction fee applies to your shipping charge too — make sure you're charging enough to cover your actual cost plus that 6.5%.

🔄

Batch your listings

The $0.20 listing fee adds up. Focus on listings that sell regularly so the fee is spread across multiple sales rather than renewals.

📊

Aim for 30%+ margin

A margin under 15% leaves no room for Etsy Ads, discounts, or slow periods. Aim for at least 30% to run a sustainable Etsy shop.

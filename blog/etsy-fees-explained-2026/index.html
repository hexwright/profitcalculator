Etsy Fees Explained 2026 — Complete Seller Guide

Etsy charges at least 4 separate fees on every single sale — and most sellers don't know all of them. This complete 2026 guide breaks down every fee (US & UK), shows you real dollar examples on a $30 product, and tells you exactly how to price for actual profit after Etsy takes its cut.

🗓 June 2026 ⏱ 7 min read 🇺🇸 US & 🇬🇧 UK fees ✅ Updated for 2026

Why Etsy Fees Surprise So Many Sellers

You list a handmade item for $30, someone buys it, and you expect to keep most of that. But by the time Etsy deducts its transaction fee, payment processing fee, listing fee — and possibly an Offsite Ads fee — you could be keeping less than $24. That's over 20% gone before your materials, packaging, or labor.

The problem isn't that Etsy fees are unreasonable. The problem is that most sellers don't know all four fee types exist, let alone how they stack. This guide changes that.

⚠️ The Most Missed Fee: Etsy charges its 6.5% transaction fee on your total sale amount including shipping. If you charge $8 shipping, Etsy takes 6.5% of that too — an extra $0.52 most sellers never account for.

All 4 Etsy Fees Explained (2026)

1. Transaction Fee — 6.5%

This is Etsy's main commission. It applies to every completed sale at 6.5% of the total amount the buyer pays — item price, shipping charge, and gift wrap combined. There is no cap and no exceptions for any seller tier.

2. Etsy Payments Processing Fee — 3% + $0.25

When a buyer pays through Etsy Payments (which is mandatory in most countries), Etsy charges a payment processing fee. The US rate is 3% + $0.25 per transaction. UK sellers pay 4% + £0.20. Eurozone sellers pay 4% + €0.30.

3. Listing Fee — $0.20 per item

Every time you publish or renew a listing, Etsy charges $0.20. Listings are active for 4 months. When a listing sells, Etsy auto-renews it for another $0.20. If you sell 3 units from one listing, that's $0.60 in listing fees on that order alone.

4. Offsite Ads Fee — 12% or 15%

Etsy runs ads on Google, Facebook, and Pinterest. If a buyer clicks one of these ads and purchases from your shop within 30 days, Etsy charges you 15% of the sale (shops under $10k/year) or 12% (shops over $10k/year, mandatory). This is the fee that shocks most sellers the first time it appears.

💡 Tip: Shops earning under $10,000/year can opt out of Offsite Ads. Go to Shop Manager → Marketing → Offsite Ads → Turn off. Shops over $10k are enrolled permanently.

How to Price on Etsy for Real Profit

Most Etsy pricing formulas you find online are dangerously incomplete. Here is the correct formula that accounts for every 2026 fee:

✅ Correct Etsy Pricing Formula:

Minimum Price = (Materials + Labor + Overhead + Target Profit) ÷ (1 − Total Fee %)

If your total Etsy fee percentage is 10% (no Offsite Ads), divide your costs by 0.90. If Offsite Ads applies at 15%, your total fee rate is approximately 25% — divide by 0.75.

Price for Worst-Case Offsite Ads

Even if you are currently opted out of Offsite Ads, your shop will be enrolled automatically if it ever crosses $10,000/year in sales. Smart sellers always price with the 12% Offsite Ads fee built in from day one — so scaling never hurts margins.

Roll Shipping Into Your Item Price

Since Etsy charges 6.5% transaction fee on shipping too, many experienced sellers offer free shipping and build the shipping cost into the item price. This simplifies your fee math and can improve your Etsy search ranking — Etsy's algorithm favors listings with free shipping.

Is Etsy Plus Worth It in 2026?

Etsy Plus costs $10/month and includes 15 listing credits ($3 value) and $5 in Etsy Ads credit. That's $8 in credits for $10 — you're effectively paying $2/month for advanced shop customization features including custom domain, banner templates, and restock alerts. For most sellers, the value is marginal unless you use Etsy Ads heavily.

5 Tips to Maximize Your Etsy Profit Margin

  1. Always calculate before you list. Use the free Etsy fee calculator before setting any price. Guessing margins is how sellers end up working for free.
  2. Opt out of Offsite Ads early. If your shop earns under $10k/year, go to Shop Manager → Marketing → Offsite Ads and turn it off until you have margins wide enough to absorb 15%.
  3. Offer free shipping and price it in. Roll your typical shipping cost into the item price. Etsy's algorithm rewards free shipping listings and buyers convert better at checkout with no surprise shipping charges.
  4. Batch similar listings. Each renewal costs $0.20. Selling 5 units from one multi-quantity listing costs $1.00. Creating 5 separate listings costs $1.00 just to list, plus $1.00 more to renew when each sells.
  5. Price for Offsite Ads from day one. Even if opted out today, build 12–15% buffer into your prices now. You may not be able to raise prices easily once buyers know your listings.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Etsy charge per sale in 2026? +
Etsy charges a 6.5% transaction fee, a $0.20 listing fee, and a 3% + $0.25 payment processing fee on every US sale. On a $30 sale, total baseline Etsy fees are approximately $3.10 — about 10.3%. If Offsite Ads applies at 15%, total fees jump to $7.60, or 25.3% of the sale price.
Does Etsy charge fees on shipping in 2026? +
Yes. Etsy's 6.5% transaction fee applies to the full amount the buyer pays, including the shipping charge. If you charge $8 for shipping, Etsy takes $0.52 of it. This is why many sellers offer free shipping and price shipping costs into the item instead.
What is Etsy's Offsite Ads fee and can I opt out? +
Offsite Ads charges 15% of the sale (under $10k/year shops) or 12% (over $10k/year, mandatory) when a buyer purchases after clicking an Etsy ad on Google, Facebook, or Pinterest. Shops earning under $10k can opt out at Shop Manager → Marketing → Offsite Ads. Over $10k/year shops cannot opt out.
Is Etsy Plus worth it in 2026? +
Etsy Plus is $10/month and includes $8 in credits (15 listing credits + $5 Ads credit). You're paying $2/month for advanced features: custom domain, banner templates, and restock alerts. For most sellers the value is marginal. High-volume sellers who use Etsy Ads daily get the most benefit.
What is a good profit margin for Etsy sellers in 2026? +
A healthy Etsy profit margin is 25–40% after all fees and costs. Baseline Etsy fees take 10–12% of revenue, so you need to price carefully. Margins below 20% leave little room for promotions, price increases, or unexpected costs. Use the free Etsy profit calculator to check your margins before listing.
How do UK Etsy seller fees differ from US fees in 2026? +
UK sellers pay the same 6.5% transaction fee and $0.20 listing fee, but Etsy Payments processing is 4% + £0.20 (vs 3% + $0.25 in the US). UK sellers also pay a Regulatory Operating Fee that varies by order value. Total UK fees are typically 1–2% higher than equivalent US sales.

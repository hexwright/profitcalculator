What Is Profit Margin in AliExpress Dropshipping?

Profit margin in dropshipping is the percentage of your selling price that you actually keep after all expenses are paid. There are two types you need to understand from the start: gross profit margin and net profit margin.

Gross profit margin is the most commonly cited figure in dropshipping tutorials. It is calculated by subtracting only the product cost (and sometimes shipping) from your selling price. If you buy a phone case from AliExpress for $3 and sell it for $15, your gross margin is 80%. This sounds great — but it means almost nothing in practice.

Net profit margin is what remains after every cost is deducted: platform fees, payment processing, advertising, returns, chargebacks, packaging inserts, and your own time. This is the number that actually determines whether your business is viable.

Net Profit Margin Formula
Net Margin (%) =
(Selling Price − Product Cost − Shipping − Platform Fees − Ad Spend − Returns & Refunds)
÷ Selling Price × 100

The gap between gross margin and net margin is where most new dropshippers go wrong. A product with a 70% gross margin might have only a 12% net margin once Facebook Ads, Shopify plan fees, and a 5% refund rate are included. Understanding this distinction is the foundation of a profitable dropshipping business.

Every Cost You Must Include in Your Margin Calculation

The most common reason dropshippers underestimate their costs is that they only count the product price and forget about the other five to eight costs that eat into every sale. Here is the complete list:

1. Product Cost

This is the price you pay to the AliExpress supplier for the item. It varies enormously — from $0.50 for a phone cable to $45 for a branded-looking gadget. Always use the supplier's price for the shipping method you will actually use, not the cheapest option that takes 60 days.

2. Shipping Cost

AliExpress offers multiple shipping options. ePacket is the most popular choice for US and UK markets — it typically costs $2–$5 and delivers in 12–20 days. AliExpress Standard Shipping is often free or very cheap but slower. For premium stores, you may use DHL or FedEx which costs $8–$20 per order but delivers in 5–7 days. Your choice of shipping directly impacts both your margin and your customer satisfaction rate (which affects returns and chargebacks).

💡 Shipping Tip Many experienced dropshippers factor in shipping costs even when the AliExpress listing says "free shipping" — because the supplier bakes shipping into the product price. Always check what comparable items cost on Alibaba in small quantities to understand the true baseline cost.

3. Platform Fees

Every platform you sell on charges fees. AliExpress itself charges a 5%–8% commission on each sale if you are using the AliExpress dropshipping program. If you are selling through Shopify, you pay a monthly plan fee ($29–$299/month) plus Shopify Payments processing at 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction. eBay charges up to 13.25% final value fee. These fees must be included in every margin calculation — not treated as a separate business overhead.

For a complete breakdown of what AliExpress charges sellers, see our guide: AliExpress Seller Fees 2026 — What You Actually Pay.

4. Advertising & Marketing Costs

This is the biggest wildcard in dropshipping margins. If you are running paid ads — Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Google Shopping — your cost per acquisition (CPA) directly determines whether a product is profitable. A $30 product with a $12 Facebook Ads CPA and a $5 product cost leaves you very little room for other fees.

As a general rule, your advertising cost should not exceed 20–30% of your selling price if you want to maintain a healthy net margin. Many new dropshippers spend 40–60% of revenue on ads during the testing phase, which turns gross margins of 60% into net losses.

5. Returns, Refunds & Chargebacks

AliExpress dropshipping has a higher-than-average refund rate due to long shipping times and the occasional quality mismatch between listing photos and actual products. A realistic refund rate to budget for is 5–10% of orders. Chargebacks (where a customer disputes the payment with their bank) can be even more costly — you lose the product cost, the sale, and often pay a $15–$25 chargeback fee to your payment processor.

⚠️ Budget for Refunds If you do not include a refund provision in your margin calculation, your actual net profit will be consistently lower than expected. Budget at least 5–8% of revenue for returns, refunds, and chargebacks.

6. Payment Processing

PayPal, Stripe, and Shopify Payments typically charge 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction for US customers. For international orders, additional cross-border fees of 1–1.5% may apply. On a $30 sale, payment processing alone costs roughly $1.17.

7. Platform Subscription & App Costs

If you use Shopify, your $29/month Basic plan adds a fixed overhead. On 50 orders per month, that is $0.58 per order — manageable. On 5 orders per month, it is $5.80 per order — a significant cost. In addition, popular dropshipping apps like DSers, AutoDS, or Zendrop cost $14–$49 per month. Email marketing tools, review apps, upsell tools — all of these add up.

In this example, the net margin is approximately 30% — which is a solid result. But notice what happens if the Facebook Ads CPA rises to $12 (which happens frequently during competitive periods or when you're still testing audiences): the net profit drops to $5.44, a net margin of just 18%. This sensitivity to ad costs is why experienced dropshippers build email lists, focus on repeat customers, and work hard to reduce CPA through better creative, better targeting, and better landing pages. Every $1 reduction in CPA adds $1 directly to your net profit per order.

💡 The 3x Rule Many profitable dropshipping stores use a simple rule: sell for at least 3× the total product + shipping cost. On a $9.70 combined cost (product + shipping), a $29.99 selling price gives a 3.1× multiple. This ensures you have enough gross margin to cover ads, fees, and refunds while still turning a profit.

Platform Comparison: Where to Sell for Best Margins

The platform you choose has a significant impact on your effective net margin. Here is how the main options compare for AliExpress dropshipping:

Shopify + Paid Ads

This is the most common and flexible setup. You control your brand, pricing, and customer experience. The downside is that you are entirely responsible for generating traffic through paid ads or SEO — there is no built-in audience. Shopify's fees are predictable ($29–$299/month + 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction), and with Shopify Payments you avoid the extra transaction fee. Best net margins possible here: 25–40% with an optimised ad account. For more information see our guide on Shopify Transaction Fees 2026.

eBay Dropshipping

eBay has a built-in audience of 130 million+ buyers, which means you can make sales without paying for ads. However, eBay's final value fee is up to 13.25% — one of the highest in the industry — and eBay's policies on dropshipping are strict: you must be able to fulfil orders within 30 days, and using AliExpress suppliers who ship in 15–25 days can risk account suspensions. Net margins on eBay dropshipping are typically 10–20% after fees. See our eBay Seller Fees 2026 guide for the full breakdown.

Etsy Dropshipping

Etsy only allows dropshipping of handmade, vintage, or craft-supply items — technically, reselling mass-produced AliExpress products is against Etsy's policies. That said, some sellers list AliExpress products as "personalised" items (adding custom engraving or print-on-demand elements). Etsy charges a $0.20 listing fee, a 6.5% transaction fee, and payment processing fees. If you can legitimately use Etsy, its niche audience and higher average order values can support margins of 20–35%. Compare the platforms in our eBay vs Etsy guide.

WooCommerce / Your Own Website

WooCommerce (on WordPress) is free to use and has no monthly platform fee, but requires hosting ($5–$30/month) and more technical setup. The benefit is zero transaction fees and complete control over your store. For sellers comfortable with WordPress, WooCommerce can add 1–3% to your net margin versus Shopify, which compounds significantly at scale.

How to Increase Your AliExpress Dropshipping Margin in 2026

If you are currently seeing net margins below 15%, these are the highest-leverage actions to improve your profitability:

1. Find Suppliers Directly on Alibaba

AliExpress is a retail marketplace — prices are retail prices. Once you are selling consistently (100+ units of one product per month), contact the supplier directly on Alibaba and negotiate a wholesale price. It is common to reduce your product cost by 30–50% this way, which can add 10–20 percentage points to your net margin.

2. Reduce Your Facebook Ads CPA

Your cost per acquisition is the single biggest lever in dropshipping profitability. Improve your ad creative quality, test multiple audiences, retarget website visitors, and build lookalike audiences from purchasers. A reduction in CPA from $10 to $7 on a $30 product adds 10 full percentage points to your net margin.

3. Build an Email List From Day One

Email marketing is the highest-ROI channel in e-commerce. Once you have an email list, you can generate repeat purchases with near-zero acquisition cost. Stores that generate 20–30% of revenue from email marketing effectively cut their blended CPA dramatically, boosting overall margins.

4. Increase Average Order Value (AOV)

Platform fees and many advertising costs are fixed per order, not per dollar. If you can raise your AOV from $25 to $45 through bundles, upsells, or volume discounts, your fixed cost percentage drops significantly. A $0.30 Shopify payment flat fee represents 1.2% on a $25 order but only 0.67% on a $45 order.

5. Choose ePacket or AliExpress Standard Shipping Strategically

AliExpress Standard Shipping has become significantly faster in 2025–2026 and is often free or $1–$2. If your product is lightweight and the delivery times are acceptable (15–20 days), switching from ePacket (which costs $3–$5) can save $2–$3 per order. On 200 monthly orders, that is $400–$600 in additional profit per month.

6. Vet Suppliers on Quality and Reliability

A supplier with consistent quality reduces your refund and chargeback rate. Moving from a 9% refund rate to a 4% refund rate on a $30 product saves $1.50 per order in average refund costs. Choose suppliers with 4.8+ star ratings, 95%+ positive feedback, and 500+ reviews on the specific item you are selling.

7. Use a Product Profit Calculator Before Listing Anything

Never list a product without running the full margin calculation first. Use the free AliExpress profit calculator at ProfitCalcu to stress-test your numbers — especially at different CPA scenarios. Only list products where your margin holds up even if your CPA is 20% higher than expected.

Is AliExpress Dropshipping Still Worth It in 2026?

The honest answer is: it depends on how you approach it. The low-effort, spray-and-pray approach of 2017–2019 — add products from AliExpress, run Facebook Ads, repeat — is no longer reliably profitable. Competition has intensified, ad costs have risen, and customers have higher expectations for shipping speed.

However, the fundamentals of the model are still sound. You can start with very little capital, test products quickly without holding inventory, and scale winners rapidly. The sellers who thrive in 2026 share a few common characteristics:

  • They spend serious time on product research and only test validated winners
  • They build real brands, not just generic stores
  • They treat their suppliers as partners and negotiate better terms over time
  • They build email lists and customer databases to reduce dependence on paid ads
  • They always know their numbers — net margin per product, blended CPA, refund rate, LTV

If you approach AliExpress dropshipping as a real business — not a get-rich-quick scheme — the model absolutely still works. Realistic expectations for a well-run store in 2026 are net margins of 20–35% and monthly profit of $1,000–$10,000+ once you have found your winning products and audiences.

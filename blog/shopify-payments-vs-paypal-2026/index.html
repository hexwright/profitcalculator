Shopify Payments vs PayPal 2026 — Which Saves You More Per Sale?

Using PayPal on Shopify can cost you up to 5.49% + $0.49 per transaction. Shopify Payments charges just 2.9% + $0.30. Here's the complete 2026 fee-by-fee breakdown with real numbers — and why the difference compounds fast at volume.

Quick Verdict: Shopify Payments wins on cost — by a wide margin

For most Shopify sellers, Shopify Payments is cheaper on every plan. Using PayPal as your only gateway adds Shopify's extra transaction fee (up to 2%) on top of PayPal's own fees — nearly doubling your per-sale payment cost. The only reason to use PayPal is as a secondary checkout option to improve conversion rate, never as your primary gateway.

One of the most common and costly mistakes Shopify sellers make in 2026 is relying on PayPal as their primary — or only — payment gateway. It feels familiar, customers trust it, and it's easy to set up. But the hidden cost of using PayPal on Shopify is Shopify's additional transaction fee, which stacks directly on top of PayPal's own processing fees.

In this guide, we break down every fee for both Shopify Payments and PayPal across all Shopify plans, show you the exact cost difference on real sale amounts, and explain the one scenario where offering PayPal still makes sense — even though it costs more.

The Key Difference: Shopify's Extra Transaction Fee

To understand why this comparison matters so much, you need to understand one important rule about how Shopify charges for payments:

📌 Shopify's Payment Gateway Rule
If you use Shopify Payments → you pay only the processing fee. No extra charge from Shopify.

If you use any third-party gateway (PayPal, Stripe, Square, etc.) → you pay the gateway's fee PLUS an extra Shopify transaction fee on every single sale.
The extra Shopify transaction fee is 2.0% (Basic), 1.0% (Shopify plan), or 0.5% (Advanced). It goes directly to Shopify — not to your payment processor.

This is Shopify's mechanism for incentivizing sellers to use its own payment system. Every dollar you process through PayPal is a dollar Shopify doesn't earn from Shopify Payments — so they charge you extra to use a competitor.

The result: PayPal on Shopify Basic can cost you nearly twice as much per transaction as Shopify Payments. Let's see exactly how.

When Should You Still Offer PayPal on Shopify?

Despite the higher cost, there is one legitimate reason to offer PayPal on your Shopify store — and it's about checkout conversion rate, not fees.

Studies consistently show that offering PayPal as an additional payment option at checkout can increase conversion rate by 3–6% for stores targeting audiences where PayPal is preferred (particularly older demographics and international buyers). A higher conversion rate means more sales — and more total profit, even if the per-sale fee is higher.

The right way to use PayPal on Shopify

1

Set Shopify Payments as your primary gateway

All card payments (Visa, Mastercard, Amex) should go through Shopify Payments. This covers the vast majority of your transactions at the lowest possible fee rate.

2

Add PayPal as a secondary option

Enable PayPal Express Checkout as an alternative for customers who prefer it. Most Shopify checkouts show it as a secondary button below the main card fields. You'll pay the extra fee only on the subset of customers who choose PayPal.

3

Monitor your PayPal order share

Track what percentage of your orders are processed through PayPal in your Shopify analytics. If it's above 20%, the conversion benefit may outweigh the cost. If it's below 10%, the extra revenue from those PayPal customers may not justify the per-sale overhead.

4

Consider Shop Pay for conversion uplift instead

Shop Pay (built into Shopify Payments) is consistently one of the highest-converting checkout options available — outperforming PayPal in many A/B tests. Enabling Shop Pay gives customers a fast one-tap checkout without triggering the extra PayPal transaction fee.

What About Shop Pay — How Does It Compare?

Shop Pay is not a separate gateway — it's a checkout accelerator that works on top of Shopify Payments. When a customer checks out using Shop Pay, the transaction is processed by Shopify Payments. This means:

  • No extra transaction fee
  • Same Shopify Payments processing rate applies
  • Faster checkout for returning customers (saved cards, autofill)
  • Access to Shop Pay Installments (buy now, pay later)
  • Higher conversion rates — Shopify reports up to 50% faster checkout and 28% higher conversion vs regular checkout

Shop Pay is, in effect, the best of both worlds: the low processing rates of Shopify Payments plus a trusted, familiar one-click checkout experience that rivals PayPal's convenience — without the extra cost.

✅
Enable Shop Pay: If you haven't already, enable Shop Pay in your Shopify admin under Settings → Payments. It's included with Shopify Payments at no extra cost and can meaningfully improve your checkout conversion rate.

What If Shopify Payments Isn't Available in My Country?

As of 2026, Shopify Payments is available in the US, UK, Canada, Australia, Ireland, New Zealand, Singapore, Hong Kong, Japan, Germany, France, Italy, Spain, Netherlands, Belgium, Sweden, Denmark, Finland, Norway, and several other markets.

If your country is not on that list — such as Pakistan, India, UAE, most of Africa, and parts of Southeast Asia — you have no choice but to use a third-party gateway. In this case, Shopify's extra transaction fee is unavoidable. Here's how to minimize its impact:

  • Upgrade your plan if volume justifies it. Moving from Basic (2.0% extra fee) to the Shopify plan (1.0% extra fee) saves 1% on every transaction. At $5,000/month in revenue, that saves $50/month — making the $66/month plan upgrade potentially cost-neutral.
  • Use the lowest-fee gateway available in your region. Compare local gateway options and choose the one with the lowest percentage fee to minimize total transaction cost.
  • Increase Average Order Value (AOV). Since the flat part of payment fees ($0.30 or $0.49) is fixed per transaction, higher-value orders mean the flat fee becomes a smaller percentage of the total.
🌍
Tip for international sellers: Even if Shopify Payments isn't available in your home country, if you're selling primarily to US or UK customers, you may be able to register a US or UK business entity and qualify for Shopify Payments. Consult a local accountant or cross-border ecommerce specialist for guidance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I use Shopify Payments or PayPal on Shopify? ▼
Use Shopify Payments as your primary gateway. It's cheaper on every plan because it avoids Shopify's extra 2.0% transaction fee that applies to all third-party gateways including PayPal. You can optionally add PayPal as a secondary checkout option to capture customers who prefer it — but most of your transactions should go through Shopify Payments.
Does Shopify charge extra if I use PayPal? ▼
Yes. Shopify charges an additional transaction fee of 2.0% (Basic), 1.0% (Shopify plan), or 0.5% (Advanced) on every sale processed through any third-party payment gateway, including PayPal. This fee goes to Shopify and is charged on top of whatever PayPal charges for the transaction.
What are PayPal's fees on Shopify in 2026? ▼
PayPal's standard fee for online transactions in 2026 is 3.49% + $0.49 (US). On top of that, Shopify adds its transaction fee. On the Basic plan, your total effective rate using PayPal is 5.49% + $0.49 per sale. On the Shopify plan it's 4.49% + $0.49, and on Advanced it's 3.99% + $0.49.
Can I use both Shopify Payments and PayPal together? ▼
Yes, and this is the recommended approach for most sellers. Set Shopify Payments as your primary gateway for card payments. Enable PayPal Express as an additional option. Card-paying customers use Shopify Payments (lower fee). Customers who prefer PayPal use PayPal (higher fee, but you capture the sale you might otherwise lose).
Is Shopify Payments available in all countries? ▼
No. As of 2026, Shopify Payments is available in roughly 20 countries including the US, UK, Canada, Australia, and most of Western Europe. Sellers in countries like Pakistan, India, UAE, and many parts of Asia, Africa, and Latin America cannot use Shopify Payments and must use a third-party gateway, paying the extra Shopify transaction fee on every sale.
What is Shop Pay and how is it different from PayPal? ▼
Shop Pay is Shopify's own accelerated checkout product. It's built on top of Shopify Payments — so transactions through Shop Pay are processed at Shopify Payments rates with no extra fee. Shop Pay saves customer card details and allows one-tap checkout. It's Shopify's answer to PayPal's one-click convenience, and studies show Shop Pay consistently outperforms PayPal in checkout conversion rates.
How do I switch from PayPal to Shopify Payments? ▼
Go to Shopify Admin → Settings → Payments. Click "Activate Shopify Payments" and complete the account setup (requires your business details, bank account, and ID verification). Once activated, Shopify Payments becomes your primary gateway and the extra Shopify transaction fee on card payments goes away immediately. You can keep PayPal as a secondary option if you want.

Final Verdict — What You Should Do Right Now

The decision is clear for the vast majority of Shopify sellers:

  • ✅ If Shopify Payments is available in your country: Enable it immediately as your primary gateway. Eliminate the extra transaction fee. Save hundreds to thousands of dollars per year depending on your volume.
  • ✅ Also enable Shop Pay: It's included with Shopify Payments and improves checkout conversion. No extra cost.
  • ✅ Add PayPal as a secondary option: Only if conversion data or your customer demographic suggests a meaningful share of your customers prefer PayPal checkout. Monitor what percentage of orders go through PayPal and decide if the conversion benefit justifies the extra cost.
  • ⚠️ If Shopify Payments isn't available in your country: Choose the lowest-fee local gateway, upgrade your plan at sufficient revenue to reduce the extra transaction fee rate, and focus on increasing AOV to dilute fixed per-transaction costs.

Switching from PayPal-only to Shopify Payments as your primary gateway is one of the fastest, highest-return optimizations a Shopify seller can make. It takes 10–15 minutes, costs nothing, and the savings compound with every single order you process from that point forward.

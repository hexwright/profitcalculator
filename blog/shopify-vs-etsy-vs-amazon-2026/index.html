Platform Snapshot — Quick Comparison 2026

Before diving into the details, here's the honest 30-second version: Etsy is a curated marketplace with built-in buyers and relatively high per-sale fees. Amazon FBA is the world's largest product search engine with massive reach but complex, expensive fees. Shopify is your own store — low fees, but zero built-in traffic. None of them is universally "best." The right answer depends on what you sell, how you get customers, and how much time you have.

Etsy in 2026 — Strengths, Weaknesses & Best Fit

Etsy is the world's largest marketplace for handmade, vintage, and unique goods, with over 90 million active buyers as of 2026. It's particularly strong in the US, UK, Germany, France, Australia, and Canada — the six markets that make up the majority of Etsy's buyer base.

Etsy's key strengths in 2026

Built-in demand. Etsy buyers arrive with purchase intent — they're actively searching for handmade candles, custom jewellery, vintage clothing, digital downloads, and craft supplies. You don't need to pay for traffic to get your first sale. For a new seller with no audience, this is Etsy's most powerful advantage.

Low barrier to entry. Opening an Etsy shop costs nothing upfront. You pay $0.20 per listing and the transaction fee only when you make a sale. Compare this to Shopify's $39/month regardless of whether you sell anything, and Etsy's risk profile for beginners is significantly lower.

Community and trust signals. Etsy's star seller badge, review system, and buyer protection build trust that a brand-new Shopify store takes months or years to develop. A customer who has never heard of your brand will buy from you on Etsy more readily because of the platform's credibility.

Etsy's weaknesses in 2026

Fee creep. Etsy's transaction fee increased from 5% to 6.5% in 2022 and hasn't decreased since. Combined with payment processing, listing fees, and potential offsite ads fees (12–15% on sales driven by Etsy's ads), the total platform take of 10–15% per sale is significantly higher than Shopify's 3–4%.

Limited brand control. Your Etsy shop lives inside Etsy's ecosystem. You can't install custom code, create landing pages, or run email automations with the sophistication of Shopify. Your customer's first loyalty is to Etsy, not to you — and Etsy can change its algorithm, policies, or fees at any time.

Increasing competition. Etsy's seller count has roughly tripled since 2019. Many product categories — especially print-on-demand, digital downloads, and jewellery — are extremely saturated. Getting found organically requires strong SEO, excellent photography, and consistent reviews.

🧡 Etsy Best For: Makers and crafters selling handmade, vintage, or truly unique products. Digital download sellers. New sellers who need traffic without upfront marketing spend. Anyone testing a product idea before building a full brand.

Amazon FBA in 2026 — Strengths, Weaknesses & Best Fit

Amazon is a different beast entirely. It's not a niche marketplace — it's the product search engine for over 300 million active customers worldwide. When someone knows they want a product but hasn't decided on a brand, they often go to Amazon first. This intent-rich traffic is Amazon's most powerful asset for sellers.

Amazon FBA's key strengths in 2026

Prime buyers and fulfillment trust. Amazon Prime members — over 200 million globally — have a deeply conditioned trust in the platform's fulfillment. The Prime badge on your listing eliminates a massive purchase friction point. FBA handles storage, picking, packing, shipping, returns, and customer service. For sellers who want to scale without building a logistics operation, this is transformative.

Massive organic reach. Amazon's internal search is one of the most powerful product discovery tools in the world. A well-optimized listing with strong reviews can generate consistent organic sales at zero incremental ad cost. Some FBA sellers run profitable businesses with minimal PPC spend.

Brand Registry and IP protection. Amazon Brand Registry gives you enhanced content (A+ pages), brand protection against hijackers, and access to Sponsored Brand ads. For serious brand builders, this is a substantial feature set unavailable on Etsy.

Amazon FBA's weaknesses in 2026

Fee complexity. Amazon's fee structure is the most complicated of the three platforms. You're paying a referral fee (8–15%), FBA fulfillment fee (size and weight based), monthly storage fee, and potential long-term storage fees. For low-price or low-margin products, these fees can make profitability nearly impossible. Our guide covers every fee in detail: Amazon FBA Fees 2026 — What Sellers Need to Know →

PPC is increasingly essential. Amazon's organic rankings are heavily influenced by sales velocity, and sales velocity is increasingly driven by Amazon PPC ads. A budget of $500–$2,000/month in Amazon ads is effectively a cost of doing business for most new FBA sellers in competitive categories. This isn't factored into the basic fee comparison, but it's real money.

Account suspension risk. Amazon suspends seller accounts — sometimes without warning and sometimes incorrectly. Losing access to your entire inventory and cash flow while an appeal grinds through Amazon's system is an existential risk that neither Etsy nor Shopify carries to the same degree.

Zero customer relationship. Amazon owns the customer. You don't get the buyer's email address. You can't retarget them. You can't build a loyalty program. Every sale resets your customer acquisition problem. This means your business is almost entirely dependent on Amazon's algorithm and policies.

📦 Amazon FBA Best For: Sellers with branded, mass-market products that compete on specification and price. High-volume sellers who want Prime fulfillment without building logistics. Experienced sellers with enough margin to absorb 25–40% platform costs. Not ideal for handmade, one-of-a-kind, or very low-margin products.

Shopify in 2026 — Strengths, Weaknesses & Best Fit

Shopify is not a marketplace. It's a platform for building your own online store. This distinction is the most important thing to understand about the Shopify vs Etsy vs Amazon comparison — Shopify doesn't give you customers. You build them yourself. That changes the entire economics of the platform.

Shopify's key strengths in 2026

Lowest per-sale fees. Using Shopify Payments on the Basic plan, your total cost per sale is approximately 2.9% + $0.30 — around 3.5–4% on a typical order. Compare that to 10–12% on Etsy or 25–40% on Amazon FBA. At scale, this fee advantage compounds into enormous profit differences. A $100,000/month business pays roughly $3,500/month in fees on Shopify vs $10,000–$12,000 on Etsy or $25,000–$40,000 on Amazon.

Full brand ownership and customer data. Every customer who buys on your Shopify store is yours. You get their email address, purchase history, and behavioral data. You can run sophisticated email marketing (Klaviyo, Shopify Email), loyalty programs, retargeting ads, SMS campaigns, and subscription offers. Over time, this owned customer relationship becomes your most valuable business asset.

Unlimited customization. Shopify's 8,000+ app ecosystem, custom theme development, and checkout extensibility mean you can build almost any ecommerce experience you can imagine. Custom product configurators, subscription billing, B2B portals, AR product previews — none of this is possible on Etsy or Amazon.

Shopify's weaknesses in 2026

Zero built-in traffic. Shopify gives you a store but no customers. You are entirely responsible for driving traffic through SEO, paid social (Meta, TikTok), Google Ads, influencer marketing, email, or any other channel. For a new seller with no existing audience or marketing budget, this is a serious obstacle that negates much of the fee advantage.

Monthly cost regardless of sales. You pay $39/month whether you sell one product or a thousand. For sellers doing under $500/month in revenue, this is a significant overhead compared to Etsy's performance-based fee model.

🟢 Shopify Best For: Brands with an existing audience, social media following, or content strategy. Sellers ready to invest in paid ads or SEO. Businesses scaling past $10,000/month where fee savings justify the traffic cost. Anyone building a long-term brand rather than a marketplace side hustle.

Traffic: The Hidden Cost Nobody Talks About

The fee comparison makes Shopify look like the obvious winner. It's not — because every comparison conveniently forgets to price in the cost of getting customers to your Shopify store. Let's fix that.

Here's what traffic typically costs in 2026 across different channels:

  • Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram): Average cost-per-purchase in most product categories runs $15–$45. A store converting at 2% with a $2 CPC is paying $100 in ads per $200 in sales — a 50% acquisition cost before any platform fees.
  • Google Shopping Ads: Average ROAS (return on ad spend) for ecommerce across industries is 3–5x. That means $1 in ad spend returns $3–$5 in revenue — or a 20–33% effective "ad fee" on every sale.
  • SEO (organic search): Free per click once you rank, but building domain authority takes 12–24 months minimum and requires consistent content investment. Not a short-term traffic strategy for new stores.
  • Email marketing: Once you have a list, email is the most cost-effective channel (often 30–40x ROI). But you need to build that list first — usually through paid ads or marketplace traffic.

With a $25 paid acquisition cost, Shopify goes from winner to money-loser on the first sale. This is why the fee comparison alone is misleading. Shopify only "wins" when:

  • You have an existing audience (email list, social following, content traffic)

Should You Sell on All Three Platforms?

The short answer for most sellers doing more than $5,000/month: yes, you probably should run at least two platforms simultaneously — and the strategic combination matters.

The most common and effective multi-channel setup in 2026 is Etsy + Shopify. Etsy provides the organic discovery engine — buyers who find you searching for "soy candles" or "leather wallets" organically. Your Shopify store is where you convert those buyers into loyal, repeat customers via email sequences and loyalty discounts. You funnel Etsy buyers toward your Shopify shop for their second purchase, where you keep 54% margin instead of 48%.

The Amazon + Shopify combination works well for brand builders in mass-market categories. Amazon provides the volume and Prime fulfilment trust. Shopify provides the owned brand experience, subscription options, and bundle sales that Amazon can't accommodate. Many 7-figure brands run 60–70% of revenue through Amazon for the volume, while using Shopify to protect margin on direct customers.

Running all three simultaneously is entirely possible with the right inventory management tools (Linnworks, Veeqo, or Shopify's native multi-channel features). The risk is operational complexity — managing listings, inventory, pricing, and customer service across three platforms requires either strong systems or a dedicated team.

💡 Multi-Channel Rule of Thumb: Start with one platform until you hit $3,000–$5,000/month consistently. Then add a second. Build systems before adding a third. Platform diversification protects against algorithm changes, policy updates, or account suspensions — which happen on all three platforms.

UK Sellers — Does the Comparison Change?

For UK-based sellers, the fundamental comparison holds — but there are important differences worth noting.

Etsy in the UK: Etsy Payments processes in GBP and the transaction fee remains 6.5%. Payment processing for UK sellers is 4% + £0.20. UK sellers should also note that since Brexit, selling to EU buyers involves VAT and customs complexity that Etsy partially handles through its Managed Payments system.

Amazon UK: Amazon.co.uk referral fees are broadly similar to the US but vary slightly by category. FBA fees are charged in GBP. UK FBA sellers should also factor in the cost of VAT registration (required once turnover exceeds the registration threshold) and the complexity of selling across Amazon.de, .fr, .it, and .es through Amazon's Pan-European FBA program.

Shopify in the UK: Shopify Payments is available in the UK, so UK sellers can avoid transaction fees just as US sellers can. The Basic plan costs £25/month on annual billing. Card processing rates for UK sellers are 2% + 25p online. The fee advantage over Etsy and Amazon is similar to the US comparison.

Final Verdict — Shopify vs Etsy vs Amazon 2026

There is no single winner. The "best" platform depends entirely on your product type, your existing audience, your sales volume, and your long-term brand goals. Here's the clearest way to think about it:

🧡 Etsy
Best Start
For handmade sellers, digital products, and new sellers with no audience. Low risk, immediate traffic, higher fees at scale.
📦 Amazon FBA
Best Volume
For branded, mass-market products with strong margins. Unmatched reach and Prime trust, but complex fees and zero customer ownership.
🟢 Shopify
Best Long-Term
For brand builders with existing traffic or a paid acquisition strategy. Lowest fees, full ownership, highest long-term brand equity.

If you're starting from zero with a handmade or unique product, start on Etsy. The built-in traffic is worth more than the fee difference at low volumes. Once you're generating consistent sales and have customer email addresses, open a Shopify store and start migrating repeat buyers there.

If you have a genuinely scalable, mass-market product with enough margin to absorb 25–35% platform costs, Amazon FBA deserves serious consideration. The Prime badge and organic reach can generate sales volume that no other platform matches — but go in with realistic fee models and a PPC budget.

If you already have an audience — a social following, an email list, or an existing blog — Shopify is likely your most profitable option from day one. The 3–4% fee structure versus 10–40% on the marketplaces represents a fundamental difference in how much of each sale you keep as the business scales.

Frequently Asked Questions — Shopify vs Etsy vs Amazon 2026

Which platform has the lowest fees — Shopify, Etsy, or Amazon?
Shopify has the lowest per-sale fees when using Shopify Payments — approximately 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction, with no transaction fee. Etsy's total take is 10–12% per sale. Amazon FBA's total cost (referral + fulfillment + storage) is typically 25–40% of the sale price. However, Shopify requires you to pay for traffic, which often costs more than the fee difference at low volumes.
Can I sell on Shopify, Etsy, and Amazon at the same time?
Yes, and many successful sellers do exactly this. Etsy provides organic marketplace discovery, Amazon handles high-volume Prime buyers, and Shopify is your owned brand hub for email marketing and repeat customers. Tools like Shopify's native sales channels, Linnworks, or Veeqo can sync inventory across all three. The main challenge is operational complexity — manage one platform well before adding more.
Is Amazon FBA worth it in 2026 for small sellers?
It depends on your product's margins and price point. Amazon FBA is generally not worth it for products under $15–20 because FBA fulfillment fees ($3.22–$5+) plus the referral fee (8–15%) consume most of the profit. For products priced $25–$60 with at least 50% gross margin before fees, Amazon FBA can be very profitable — especially if you're selling a branded product that can build reviews and organic rank.
Which is better for a beginner — Etsy or Shopify?
Etsy is better for most beginners because it provides immediate traffic without a marketing budget. You can open a shop, list products, and potentially make a sale within days — something that takes months on a new Shopify store. Shopify is better for beginners who already have an audience (social media, email list, blog) or who are ready to invest in paid ads from day one.
How does Amazon FBA compare to Etsy for handmade products?
Etsy is overwhelmingly better for genuine handmade products. Amazon's Handmade section exists but has far less buyer traffic and less brand credibility for artisan goods. Etsy buyers specifically seek out handmade, unique items and pay a premium for them. Amazon buyers typically prioritize price, Prime delivery speed, and review count — metrics that disadvantage small handmade sellers.
At what sales volume does Shopify become more profitable than Etsy?
The break-even depends on your traffic cost. If you have free traffic (existing audience, SEO, social following), Shopify is more profitable almost immediately for stores doing $1,000+/month because the 6–8% fee saving outweighs the $39 plan fee. If you need paid ads, the break-even is typically $30,000–$60,000 in annual sales — the point where fee savings exceed typical customer acquisition costs.
Does Etsy charge fees on shipping?
Yes — this is one of Etsy's most misunderstood fees. Etsy's 6.5% transaction fee applies to the total order amount including shipping. If you charge $5 for shipping on a $30 sale, Etsy charges 6.5% on $35 ($2.28), not just on $30 ($1.95). This adds up significantly for sellers charging real shipping rates.

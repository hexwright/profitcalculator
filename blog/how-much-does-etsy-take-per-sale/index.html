How Much Does Etsy Take
Per Sale in 2026?

On a $25 sale, Etsy keeps around $4–$5 before you even count shipping or production costs. Most sellers only know about the 6.5% transaction fee — but there are actually 4 separate fees Etsy charges on every sale. This guide breaks down every single one.

✍️ ProfitCalcu Team 📅 June 4, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read 🇺🇸 US & 🇬🇧 UK
⚡ Quick Answer

On a $25 sale (no shipping), Etsy takes approximately $4.08–$5.33 depending on Offsite Ads. That's 16%–21% of your sale price going to Etsy before COGS or other costs. The main fees are: 6.5% transaction fee + $0.20 listing fee + 3%+$0.25 payment processing + optional 15% Offsite Ads fee.

Etsy Transaction Fee — 6.5% Explained

The 6.5% transaction fee is Etsy's main revenue source from sellers. It's charged on the total sale price including shipping — so if you charge $5 shipping separately, Etsy also takes 6.5% of that $5.

This fee was raised from 5% to 6.5% in April 2022 and has not changed since. There is no cap — if you sell a $500 item, Etsy takes $32.50 in transaction fees alone.

⚠️ Important: The 6.5% transaction fee applies to your shipping charge as well, not just the item price. Many sellers forget this when pricing their listings.

Etsy Listing Fee — $0.20 Per Item

Every time you create a new listing or sell an item, Etsy charges a $0.20 listing fee (£0.16 in the UK). This fee renews every time:

  • You sell an item (one fee per unit sold)
  • Your listing expires after 4 months with no sale

For high-volume sellers, listing fees add up fast. If you sell 200 items per month, that's $40/month in listing fees alone.

Etsy Payment Processing Fee — 3% + $0.25

This fee covers Etsy Payments (the built-in checkout). In the US it's 3% + $0.25 per transaction. In the UK it's 4% + £0.20. Other countries have different rates.

Unlike the transaction fee, the payment processing fee is charged on the total order amount including tax in some regions — so your actual deduction can be slightly higher than calculated.

Offsite Ads Fee — 12% or 15%

Etsy automatically promotes your listings on Google, Facebook, Instagram, and Pinterest through Offsite Ads. If a buyer clicks one of these ads and then purchases within 30 days, Etsy charges you an additional fee:

  • 15% — for sellers who made less than $10,000 in the past 12 months
  • 12% — for sellers who made over $10,000 (mandatory, cannot be turned off)

If your shop is under $10,000/year, you can opt out of Offsite Ads in your Shop Settings → Marketing → Offsite Ads.

Tips to Reduce What Etsy Takes Per Sale

  • Turn off Offsite Ads if your shop earns under $10,000/year — saves 15% on ad-driven sales
  • Include shipping in your item price — customers prefer free shipping and you control the math better
  • Avoid relisting unsold items frequently — each relist costs $0.20
  • Use the Etsy calculator before pricing — know your margins before you list, not after
  • Bundle items — one transaction fee instead of multiple small fees

Etsy Fees in 2026 — Summary

To answer the question directly: Etsy takes between 10%–22% of your sale price depending on your situation. For most sellers without Offsite Ads on a typical $25–$50 product, expect Etsy to take around 11%–13% of the sale price in combined fees.

The key is to always calculate your profit after all fees before you set your price — not after you've already listed.

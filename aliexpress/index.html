How to Use the AliExpress Fee Calculator

1

Enter Sale Price & Shipping

Type your AliExpress product price and any shipping amount you charge the customer. The payment processing fee applies to the total amount collected, so including shipping gives you accurate results.

2

Select Product Category

Choose your product category. AliExpress commission rates range from 5% (Electronics) to 8% (Clothing, Beauty). Your category determines the commission deducted on every sale. Higher-margin categories can absorb the higher fee.

3

Add Payment Rate & Your Costs

Select the payment processing rate (2.4%–3.4%) for your region and buyer method. Then enter your product cost, shipping cost, and marketing spend to see your true net profit and margin.

Frequently Asked Questions — AliExpress Seller Fees 2026

AliExpress charges a category-based commission (5%–8%) plus a payment processing fee (2.4%–3.4%) on every completed sale. On a $50 sale of Clothing, the total fee is approximately $5.45 (10.9%). On Electronics, it's approximately $3.95 (7.9%). Use this calculator to see the exact amount for your category and price.
AliExpress commission is a percentage fee deducted from each completed sale before the remaining amount is released to the seller's account. The rate depends entirely on the product category — from 5% for Electronics to 8% for Clothing and Beauty. This commission is AliExpress's primary revenue from sellers and is applied automatically; there's no way to reduce it without switching product categories.
AliExpress category commission applies to the product sale price only. However, the payment processing fee (2.4%–3.4%) applies to the total amount collected from the buyer, which includes any shipping you charge. To simplify your fee calculations, enter your shipping charge in the "Shipping Charged to Customer" field in this calculator for accurate results.
AliExpress offers access to a global marketplace with hundreds of millions of buyers across 200+ countries. Combined fees of 7.9%–11.4% are competitive with most e-commerce platforms. The key is pricing products with enough margin to cover fees, shipping, and product cost. AliExpress works best for products with at least 30–40% gross margin before fees, leaving room for profit after all deductions.
From a fees perspective, Electronics (5% commission) has the lowest rate, but margins on electronics tend to be thinner. Home & Garden (6%) offers a balance of moderate fees and good product variety. Clothing and Beauty have the highest commission (8%) but also attract the most buyers and allow for significant markup. A healthy AliExpress profit margin is 20–35% net after all fees and costs. With combined AliExpress fees running 7.9%–11.4% of sale price, plus product cost and shipping, you need products with enough markup. Margins below 15% leave little buffer for returns, disputes, and ad spend. Use this calculator to model your pricing before listing products.
AliExpress profit = Sale Price − Category Commission − Payment Processing Fee − Product Cost − Shipping Cost − Ad Spend. For example, on a $50 Clothing item with $12 product cost and $5 shipping: $50 − $4.00 (8%) − $1.45 (2.9%) − $12 − $5 = $27.55 net profit (55.1% margin). Tips to Maximize Your AliExpress Profit Margin

📂

List in the lowest-commission category that fits

If your product legitimately fits in Electronics (5%) vs. Clothing (8%), listing in the lower-commission category saves 3% on every sale. On $10,000/month revenue that's $300/month — real money. Always check which categories your products qualify for.

💸

Price above $30 to dilute the fixed impact

AliExpress payment processing fees are percentage-based (no fixed amount), so the fee structure is proportional. However, higher-priced items give you more absolute margin per sale to cover your costs. Bundle or upsell products to increase your average order value.

🚢

Optimize shipping costs with AliExpress Standard Shipping

AliExpress Standard Shipping (AliExpress Logistics) often offers better rates than commercial couriers for cross-border orders. Lower shipping costs increase your net margin directly. Negotiate rates with suppliers who can ship via this network for you.

⭐

Maintain high store ratings to avoid fee penalties

Sellers with high ratings and good dispute resolution records face fewer payment holds and transaction delays. A poor rating can lead to delayed payouts, increasing your effective cost of capital. Aim for 4.5+ store ratings and under 5% dispute rate.
